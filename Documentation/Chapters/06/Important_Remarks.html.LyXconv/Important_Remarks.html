Chapter 1
Important remarks and common pitfalls

Not all symbols are equally reliable. Some may be very sensitive to temporary occlusion, whereas others may give counter-intutive results to the beginner. Below is a list of the most common pitfalls and misunderstandings regarding the OpenHoldem symbols. There are for sure others, and maybe we are not even aware of them, as the issues are too obvious and natural for experienced users.

Whenever there are unclear questions regarding some symbols, ManualMode and the debug-tab will give you quick answers in a reliable and reproducable way. If you have further questions or if the results at ManualMode seem to be wrong, feel free to ask at the forums and provide some screenshots for explanantion.

  • Not all symbols are equally reliable. Some symbols do rely only on the current state (e.g. stacks and bets), some require the complete game-history (e.g. nbetsroundX). All symbols which rely on history are especially volatile against (incorrect) hand-resets and temporary occlusion. That is no bug, but simply inevitable. It is the users duty to care about a stable environment and choose a proper handreset-method for the tablemap.
  • Autoplayer-symbols (e.g. didcallroundX) mean: what the autoplayer did, not what happened in the game. If you connect in the middle of a game, if you interact manually or if you connect multiple bots to the same table, then these symbols will return unexpected results. It may also sometimes (very rarely) happen, that the autoplayer attempts to act, but that the action fails for whatever reason and has no effect. If you need failsafe code, you should check a combination of various symbols (e.g. bets and balances in combination with autoplayer-symbols).
  • Some symbols like nplayersdealt have been very sensitive to fast folds in early position, as there is no guarantee, that we catch every single game-state and every single event. The “obvious” workaround was to use nchairsdeltleft and nchairsdealtright to determine the position relative to the dealer. But as too many newbies have been overburdened by such subtleties we do now consider players as “dealt”, when they are posting blinds. This may very rarely give incorrect results, but is far better than the old approach.
  • Detecting blinds correctly from screenshots is a nearly impossible task, if you want to consider all possible cases (missing small blind; additional blinds; players posting blinds + dead; games with antes; distinguishing blinds in early position from open-limpers; players sitting in again without having to post a blind and limping; players being allin for the blind or less; reversed blinds for most heads-up games; 3-handed-games becoming headsup; etc.). Therefore OH does mostly assume, that there are exactly two blinds present in the game.
  • If there are multiple ways to make the same hand (e.g. KT on a board of 9TJQ2) OpenHoldem will first use your hole-cards, then the board-cards. In the example above it will use both cards for the straight (npcbits = 2), though one of the cards is on the board, too. Both ways have its pros and cons, but this one is the official way, as it is backward compatible to WinHoldem.
